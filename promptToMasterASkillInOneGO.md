## DELIVERY FORMAT

### Structure Your Explanation As:

1. ✓ **The Misconception** (What's confusing) — 30 seconds
2. ✓ **The Origin Story** (Why humans care) — 2-3 minutes
3. ✓ **The Core Principle** (The ONE thing) — 1 paragraph
4. ✓ **The Mechanism** (Step-by-step + diagram) — 3-5 minutes
5. ✓ **Why This Design** (The reasoning) — 1-2 minutes
6. ✓ **The Three-Brain Connection** (Why it matters) — 2 minutes
7. ✓ **Three Examples** (Personal, Observable, Global) — 3-5 minutes
8. ✓ **The Surprise** (What's counterintuitive) — 1 minute
9. ✓ **Common Mistakes** (Fix misconceptions) — 2 minutes
10. ✓ **The Connection** (Links to other knowledge) — 1-2 minutes
11. ✓ **Practice Question** (Test understanding) — 2-3 minutes

---

### Language & Tone Requirements

- ✓ Use 10th grade vocabulary (simple words, short sentences)
- ✓ Avoid jargon (if you use it, explain immediately)
- ✓ Lots of white space and headers
- ✓ Make it scannable and easy to follow
- ✓ Use analogies liberally (the stranger, the more memorable)
- ✓ Make the person feel SMART, not intimidated

---

### Visual & Memory Requirements

- ✓ Use multiple modes: Story, Diagram, Logic, Concrete Examples, Active Practice
- ✓ Information encoded in 4+ ways = 4x better retention
- ✓ More brain regions involved = stronger memory

---

### Brain Engagement Checklist

- ✓ **Reptilian Brain:** Survival stakes, concrete language, fast direct sentences
- ✓ **Mammalian Brain:** Stories about real people, emotional language, human connection
- ✓ **Neocortex:** Logic, patterns, principles, systems thinking
- ✓ **Attention:** Story → Mechanism → Surprise → Practice (maintains engagement)

# 📋 HOW TO USE THESE PROMPTS

## Quick Start

**Copy either prompt above and replace `[INSERT TOPIC HERE]` with your topic.**

**Examples:**
- `[INSERT TOPIC HERE]` → `How Photosynthesis Works`
- `[INSERT TOPIC HERE]` → `Why Companies Compete`
- `[INSERT TOPIC HERE]` → `How Money Creates Value`
- `[INSERT TOPIC HERE]` → `Why People Procrastinate`

---

## Best Practice: Use Both Together

| Step | Action | Prompt |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Get the big picture | PROMPT 1 (Categorization) |
| 2 | Understand each part deeply | PROMPT 2 (Deep Explanation) |
| 3 | Zoom back out | PROMPT 1 (See how parts connect) |
| 4 | Practice explaining | PROMPT 2 (Check understanding) |

---

## For Teaching Others

1. **Start with PROMPT 1 output** 
   - Give them the map/structure
   - They see how everything fits

2. **Then use PROMPT 2 for each section**
   - Go deep into each component
   - They understand WHY each part exists

3. **End with PROMPT 1**
   - Show the whole picture again
   - They see the integration

---

## Customization Tips

- **For visual learners:** Emphasize diagrams and examples (PROMPT 1)
- **For logical learners:** Emphasize mechanisms and first principles (PROMPT 2)
- **For emotional learners:** Emphasize stories and significance (PROMPT 2, Step 1)
- **For kinesthetic learners:** Emphasize practice questions and concrete examples (PROMPT 2, Step 7 & 11)

---
