# Stage 1 — Create Attention

Nothing happens until attention moves.

Human attention naturally goes toward:

* threat
* opportunity
* status
* novelty
* uncertainty

---

## Example

Two headlines:

Headline A:

> New Financial Report Released

Headline B:

> 90% Of Investors Make This Mistake

Which gets attention?

B.

Why?

Threat.

---

Master lesson:

You don't control behavior first.

You control attention first.

---

# Stage 2 — Activate Emotion

After attention comes emotion.

Emotion is fuel.

Without emotion:

No movement.

---

Example:

A founder says:

> Our software is 20% faster.

Nobody cares.

Another says:

> Your competitors are already using AI.

Emotion:

Fear.

Now people move.

---

Master lesson:

People rarely move because of facts.

They move because facts trigger emotions.

---

# Stage 3 — Trigger Identity

This is where behavior becomes powerful.

Humans protect identity more than truth.

---

Examples:

"I'm a good parent."

"I'm a smart engineer."

"I'm successful."

"I'm patriotic."

"I'm ethical."

---

When identity enters:

Logic weakens.

Emotion strengthens.

---

Master lesson:

The strongest behavior is identity-driven behavior.

---

# Stage 4 — Narrative Construction

Brain cannot tolerate uncertainty.

It creates stories.

---

Situation:

Boss ignores message.

Reality:

Unknown.

---

Average person creates:

"He hates me."

Another person creates:

"He's busy."

Same event.

Different story.

Different behavior.

---

Master lesson:

Humans don't react to reality.

Humans react to stories about reality.

---

# Stage 5 — Decision

Now the brain chooses.

But the decision is already heavily biased.

Most decisions are made emotionally.

Then justified logically.

---

Example:

Buy luxury watch.

Real reason:

Status.

Stated reason:

Craftsmanship.

---

Decision feels rational.

Often it isn't.

---

# Stage 6 — Action

Behavior appears.

This is the only part most people see.

---

They observe:

* purchase
* vote
* investment
* relationship

But those are outputs.

Not causes.

---

Master lesson:

Behavior is evidence.

Not explanation.

---

# Stage 7 — Outcome

Action creates result.

---

Example:

Impulsive purchase.

Result:

Temporary pleasure.

---

Brain notices reward.

Now reinforcement begins.

Repeat.

Years pass.

Loop remains.
