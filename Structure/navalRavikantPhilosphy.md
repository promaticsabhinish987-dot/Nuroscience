## Core Orientation

Naval Ravikant operates at the intersection of **wealth creation, leverage, and inner peace**. His philosophy is unusually cohesive because it blends:

* Startup thinking (high leverage, asymmetry)
* Stoicism & Eastern philosophy (detachment, awareness)
* First-principles reasoning (strip everything to fundamentals)

---

# 1. Wealth ≠ Money ≠ Status

### Definitions (very precise)

* **Money** → medium of exchange (fungible, replaceable)
* **Status** → relative social ranking (zero-sum game)
* **Wealth** → assets that earn while you sleep (positive-sum)

### His key insight:

> “Play **positive-sum games** (wealth), avoid **status games**.”

### Practical implication:

* Don’t chase titles → chase ownership
* Don’t trade time → build systems

---

# 2. Leverage is the Real Multiplier

He categorizes leverage into **three non-linear types**:

### 1. Code

* Scales infinitely (software, automation)

### 2. Media

* Content, writing, audience

### 3. Capital

* Money invested in systems/people

### Why this matters:

> Humans historically had labor leverage → now we have **permissionless leverage**

Meaning:

* No gatekeepers required (internet era)
* Anyone can scale output massively

---

# 3. Specific Knowledge

This is one of his most misunderstood ideas.

### Definition:

> Knowledge that is:

* Hard to train
* Comes from curiosity/play
* Unique to you

### NOT:

* Generic skills (like basic coding, unless uniquely applied)

### Example:

* 1000 coders exist → not special
* Combining **coding + UX + storytelling + niche domain** → specific knowledge

### Core rule:

> Follow what feels like “play” to you but “work” to others

---

# 4. Accountability + Ownership = Wealth

He emphasizes:

* Take **accountability under your own name**
* Own **equity**, not salary

### Why:

* Society rewards **visible risk-taking**
* You need **skin in the game**

---

# 5. Judgment > Hard Work

Hard work alone is linear.

### His equation:

```
Wealth = Specific Knowledge + Accountability + Leverage + Judgment
```

### Insight:

* Better decisions → exponential outcomes
* Judgment comes from:

  * Experience
  * Reflection
  * Mental models

---

# 6. Happiness Philosophy (Very Different Layer)

Naval treats happiness like a **skill**, not a result.

### Key ideas:

#### 1. Happiness = absence of desire

* Desire = contract you make with yourself to be unhappy

#### 2. Peace > Pleasure

* Pleasure is temporary
* Peace is stable baseline

#### 3. Learn to be alone

* If you can’t sit alone → you’re dependent on external validation

---

# 7. Mental Models He Uses

### 1. Long-term games with long-term people

* Compounding trust

### 2. Read → Think → Rest

* Input → process → clarity

### 3. “Earn with your mind, not your time”

* Shift from labor → leverage

# 8. Time Philosophy

He values **time over money**:

* Buy back time aggressively
* Avoid obligations that reduce freedom

> “You want to be rich and anonymous, not poor and famous.”

---

# 9. Learning Strategy

* Read foundational material (not trends)
* Build **judgment, not just knowledge**
* Avoid news → consume timeless ideas

---

# 10. His View on Society

* Internet enables **individual sovereignty**
* Small teams > large orgs
* Permissionless systems win

---

# 11. Criticism / Reality Check

His philosophy works best if:

* You’re in a leverage-friendly domain (tech, media, capital markets)
* You can tolerate uncertainty

Limitations:

* Not directly applicable to all economic classes
* Assumes access to digital leverage

* Build or ship something scalable

### Weekly:

### Long-term:

* Stack unique skills into **specific knowledge**
